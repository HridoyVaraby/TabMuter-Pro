# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and respectful environment for everyone, regardless of experience, identity, background, or ability.

## Expected behavior

- Be considerate, patient, and constructive.
- Discuss ideas and code without attacking people.
- Give actionable feedback and accept feedback professionally.
- Respect different viewpoints and levels of experience.
- Protect private information shared during support or security discussions.
- Take responsibility for mistakes and work to repair harm.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention.
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or repeated bad-faith arguments.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and other project spaces. It also applies when someone publicly represents the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove contributions that violate this policy and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and focused on community safety.

Report conduct concerns privately to a maintainer using a contact method shown on the maintainer's GitHub profile. Do not include sensitive personal information in a public issue. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially and promptly as practical.
